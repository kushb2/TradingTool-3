📡

Step 01 — Scan & Confirm

Monitor Netweb daily on a 15-min or 1-hour chart. Confirm the 7-day cycle is still active — mark the prior swing highs and lows. Only trade when the repeating structure is clearly visible.

PRE-TRADE CHECK
🎯

Step 02 — Entry at Support

Place a limit buy order precisely at the lower boundary of the short-term price channel (support). Never chase mid-cycle momentum. Wait for price to come to you.

LIMIT BUY ONLY
✅

Step 03 — Set Take-Profit

Immediately after entry, place a rigid limit sell at +5% above your entry price. This sits comfortably inside the 7–10% historical range, maximising fill probability before resistance hits.

+5% LIMIT SELL
🛡️

Step 04 — Hard Stop-Loss

Place a hard stop-loss just below the support level. This is your mechanical invalidation trigger — if the pattern fails, you exit automatically with a small, controlled loss.

HARD STOP · NO EXCEPTIONS
⚖️

Step 05 — Position Sizing

Risk no more than 1–2% of your total capital per trade. Calculate your position size based on the distance between entry and stop-loss. Never let a single trade threaten your account.

1–2% RISK PER TRADE
🚪

Step 06 — Exit Discipline

If the +5% target is not hit by Day 7 (end of cycle), close the trade at market — the pattern window has closed. Do not hold into the next cycle hoping to recover.

TIME-STOP: DAY 7

⚠ Non-Negotiable Rules
🔒
Never Enter Mid-Cycle
If price has already bounced off support and moved up, the opportunity is gone. Wait for the next cycle's support test. Chasing momentum kills the edge.
🚫
No Moving the Stop-Loss
Once placed, the stop is fixed. Never widen it to "give the trade more room." The stop is the pattern invalidation level — period.
🕰️
Honour the Time-Stop (Day 7)
If your limit target has not been filled by end of Day 7, close at market. The statistical window has closed. Holding longer means you're now speculating, not trading a pattern.
📉
Monitor Pattern Health Weekly
This is a temporary anomaly. After each cycle, verify the pattern is still intact. If two consecutive cycles fail to make a 5% swing, stop trading it immediately.
💰
Capital Risk Cap: 1–2% Per Trade
Even a high-probability pattern fails. Size each position so that hitting your stop-loss costs you at most 1–2% of total trading capital — never more.

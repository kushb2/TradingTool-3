Step 3 — Moving Your Safety Net Up as Price Rises

Think of your safety net like a ratchet — it only moves up, never down. Once you've made some profit, you move it up to protect those gains. Here's exactly when and where to move it:

🔴 Before the trade starts
Set safety net at −4.5% below support. This is your hard floor — the pattern has clearly broken if it falls here.
HARD STOP
🟢 After R1 hit (+3%)
Move safety net up to your average buy price. You now cannot lose money on the trade — the worst case is breaking even.

🕰 Day 7 arrives
Sell everything remaining at market price, no matter what. The cycle window is closed. Walk away clean.

The Golden Rules — Read These Before Every Trade
01
Never buy if the price has already moved up
If the price has already bounced off support and risen by 2–3%, that opportunity is gone. Wait for the next wave. Buying late is the #1 beginner mistake.
02
Never remove your safety net (stop-loss)
It can only move up — never down or away. If the market is falling and you think "let me just wait a bit more," that's the exact moment you must trust the stop.
03
Day 7 is an absolute deadline — no exceptions
The 7-day window is why this pattern works. If you hold into Day 8, Day 9… you're no longer trading the pattern — you're just guessing.
04
If 2 cycles in a row fail to reach R2, stop trading it
This is a temporary pattern. Markets change. If the wave stops forming, don't force it. Step back, observe for 2–3 weeks, and only re-enter when the cycle re-establishes itself.
05
Risk only 1–2% of your total money per trade
Even a great strategy loses sometimes. Keep each bet small enough that a bad trade is a minor setback, not a disaster. Consistency over big swings.
